Horizon View
Basic Overview:
This is an interface to help you find the specific times of different astrological events.
- Sunrise
- Sunset
- Noon
- Civil Twilights
- Nautical Twilights
- Astronomical Twilights
Diagram Description:
The order of the astrological events starting at night is astronomical twilight, also known as dawn. Then nautical twilight, civil twilight, sunrise, noon, sunset, civil twilight , nautical twilight, and finally astronomical twilight, this time also known as dusk.
Degrees from Horizon:
Each of these astrological events correspond to the position of the sun relative to the horizon at the location. Sunrise and sunset are when the sun is exactly at the horizon, or at 0 degrees. Civil twilight is when the sun is 6 degrees below the horizon, nautical twilight is when the sun is 12 degrees below the horizon, and finally astronomical twilight is when the sun is 18 degrees below the horizon.
